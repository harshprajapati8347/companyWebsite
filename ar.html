Augmented • Be Innovative

Augmented reality (AR) is a live, direct or indirect view of a physical, real-world environment whose elements are augmented (or supplemented) by computer-generated sensory input such as sound, video, graphics or GPS data.

Augmented Reality
The future is immersive

Augmented reality is overlaying the camera view of your smartphone, tablet, PC or connected glasses with virtual, data, objects or other visualizations in real time.

AR offers entrepreneurs a way to thoroughly enhance day-to-day business operations; whether it’s hosting meetings or showcasing products, this developing technology holds great promise for revamping the way we think about business.

AR makes it possible to see a product in its environment before it’s completed. This value proposition is for instance applicable to furniture manufacturers, who can show their product in a potential customer living room.

AR Application

Augmented reality is enriching the real world with digital information and media, such as 3D models and videos. It is less immersive than VR, but lets the user see graphical enhancements to real, physical environment, rather than creating an entirely new environment.

Image Recognition

AR can be used for experiential marketing, training, learning, healthcare, teleporting (with a proven positive impact on survivors of PTSD) and mind-training applications.One of the key success factors for Image Recognition is how perfect it can be, and this in turn is a function of the hardware/software used.
